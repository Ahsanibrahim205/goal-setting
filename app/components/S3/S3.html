Another technique is Force Field Analysis

  • Force Field Analysis is a useful technique for looking at all the forces for and against a wellness goal that you are considering. It helps you to weigh the importance of these factors and decide whether a plan is worth implementing. When you have decided to carry out a plan, Force Field Analysis helps you identify changes that you could make to improve it.
  • The Force Field Analysis model is built on the idea that forces – such as personal habits, customs, attitudes, beliefs, values – both drive and restrain personal change.
  • To carry out a Force Field Analysis take a sheet of paper and write down a description of
    1. The current situation
    2. The ideal situation
    3. Where the current situation will go if no action is taken
    4. All the forces driving change towards your ideal situation
    5. All the restraining forces
    6. Allocate a score to each of the forces using a numerical scale e.g. (1) extremely weak (10) extremely strong
    7. Take another sheet of paper and write the ideal situation at the top, create two columns headed “enablers” and “hindrances”
    8. Chart the forces and their scores, the driving forces on the left and restraining forces on the right
    9. The chart allows you to visualise the forces at work and determine whether change is viable and progress can occur
    10. The visibility of the change programme can be affected by decreasing the strength of the restraining forces or by increasing the strength of the driving forces.
  • By carrying out the analysis you can plan to strengthen the forces supporting a decision and reduce the impact of opposition to it.

    • Now prepare a strategy for achieving your goal.



  • Remember
    1. That if you increase any of the driving forces, you may create greater resistance – for example, in other people who may be involved.
    2. Change is most easily accepted when it requires a minimum of effort and disruption.
  • Can you
    1. Maintain driving forces (enablers), and reduce any of the restraining forces (hindrances)?
    2. Find new driving forces (enablers)?
    3. Divert any restraining forces (hindrances) to new targets or in new directions?
    4. if you must drive more, choose forces that don’t increase resistance?

    • Choose a wellness goal that is important to you, that you are most committed to achieving and apply these techniques to it. You will then have two powerful goal setting techniques to use. Choose to use both or the one that is the more powerful for you at any point in time.
